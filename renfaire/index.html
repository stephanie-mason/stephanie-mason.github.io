Sewing our Smocks

When

Tentative - Sunday, April 3 at 3:30 pm

Where

Stephanie's crafting space - 3233 Greenwood Ave in the Garage

Goal of Session

Start with the foundations. Draft smock patterns, cut them out pieces, understand construction and learn any necessary (historical or modern) sewing techniques. If time, baste pattern to check fit.

At the end of this session I'd like to have everyone to the point where they have a smock cut out and are confident they have the skills to sew it together in their free time.

Before the Session

  • Pre-wash/pre-shrink white linens
  • Take measurements

Provided

Stephanie will have the following items at the crafting space ready to go

  • All of the pre-washed white linen
  • White thread to match
  • Hand sewing needles
  • Sewing machine
  • Sewing scissors/rotary cutter
  • Yard sticks
  • Cutting mat
  • Fabric markers/chalk
  • The Tudor Tailor
  • Other miscellaneous sewing notions

Bring

Bring the following with you

  • (Optional) Colorful thread for contrast seams
  • Additional sewing supplies (your own scissors, needles, etc.)
  • Examples of any style of shirt or smock you'd like to make if it isn't a standard, plain, square neck smock

Cursing the Corset (aka Building our Bodies)

When

TBD

Where

TBD

Goal of Session

Let's get this over with! We will cut out our 'bodies' (the Elizabethan word for corset) and start sewing the channels for the boning this session.

Bodies in this period were meant to be seen, and can and should be in bright or colorful fabric. If using a fancier or more delicate fabric, use a sturdier plain fabric for the lining.

Bring

  • A generated corset pattern, printed (or any corset pattern)
  • Recommended yardage (probably about 2 yds) from above pattern of corset fabric (canvas, linen, or something fancier.)
  • Boning (recommendations
  • Sewing supplies
