A Series of Main Events

As we are all busy gentle women, I propose the following outline for a crafting schedule.

Each session shall be no more brief than two hours, and no longer than three. Each shall be a focused crafting gathering, for to take advantage of the tools and expertise that each of us may not individually posess.

Bodices and Bodies

When

Friday, March 29 at 7:00pm

Where

Stephanie's crafting space - 3233 Greenwood Ave in the Garage

Goals of Session

Since we are waiting on materials, we'll use this time to do some drafting. The corset and bodice patterns are fairly similar, and have similar fitting needs so maybe we can feed two bird with one scone this session and make patterns for both.

We also need to schedule the next series of meetings, so bring your calendar!

  • Take & record measurements
  • Draft corset patterns
  • Cut out muslin toile of corsets to check fit
  • If time, draft & make toile for bodice patterns

Provided

  • Drafting paper
  • Pens/markers
  • Measuring tape
  • Scissors (fabric & paper)
  • Muslin for toiles
  • The Tudor Tailor
  • Sewing machine
  • Sewing notions (needles, thread)

Bring

  • A loose t-shirt to wear (as a substitute for a smock)
  • Your Calendar!
  • An extra pair of paper and/or fabric scissors, if you have them
  • Any sewing supplies you think may be handy (extra needles, thread snips, etc)

Sewing our Smocks - Evening of April 14th

When

Saturday, April 14th - come as early as 5pm, or as soon as is sensible for you

Where

Stephanie's crafting space - 3233 Greenwood Ave in the Garage

Goal of Session

Draft smock patterns, cut them out pieces, understand construction and learn any necessary (historical or modern) sewing techniques. If time, baste pattern to check fit.

At the end of this session I'd like to have everyone to the point where they have a smock cut out and are confident they have the skills to sew it together in their free time.

Before the Session

  • Pre-wash/pre-shrink white linens
  • Take measurements

Provided

Stephanie will have the following items at the crafting space ready to go

  • All of the pre-washed white linen
  • White thread to match
  • Hand sewing needles
  • Sewing machine
  • Sewing scissors/rotary cutter
  • Yard sticks
  • Cutting mat
  • Fabric markers/chalk
  • The Tudor Tailor
  • Other miscellaneous sewing notions

Bring

Bring the following with you

  • (Optional) Colorful thread for contrast seams
  • Additional sewing supplies (your own scissors, needles, etc.)
  • Examples of any style of shirt or smock you'd like to make if it isn't a standard, plain, square neck smock

Building our Bodies Pt 2 - Evening of April 26th

When

Friday, April 26th at 7pm

Where

Erica's House

Goal of Session

Begin construction on the corsets. Use the toile's we made in our first session to cut out the linen pieces. Draw the outlines for the boning. Begin sewing the boning channels to test the fit for the reeds. Cut reeds to size, or at least cut the roll so everyone has some to take home.

Bring

  • Your prewashed, dyed fabric (whatever color(s) you're making the corset out of).
  • You may want to iron it ahead of time as well.
  • Toile & Paper pattern from last corset session
  • Sewing shears, if you have them
