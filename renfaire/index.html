Each session shall be no more brief than two hours, and no longer than three. Each shall be a focused crafting gathering, for to take advantage of the tools and expertise that each of us may not individually posess.

Sewing our Smocks - Evening of April 14th

When

Saturday, April 14th - come as early as 5pm, or as soon as is sensible for you

Where

Stephanie's crafting space - 3233 Greenwood Ave in the Garage

Goal of Session

Draft smock patterns, cut them out pieces, understand construction and learn any necessary (historical or modern) sewing techniques. If time, baste pattern to check fit.

At the end of this session I'd like to have everyone to the point where they have a smock cut out and are confident they have the skills to sew it together in their free time.

  • Sewing scissors/rotary cutter

  • Cutting mat

Bring

  • Corset toiles

Goal of Session

Use paper corset patterns to create bodice patterns. Make & fit toiles. Make patterns for bumrolls. If time permits, cut out the bumrolls.

Intermission

There are a few weekends where we will not be able to meet. During this time, prioritize finishing things in this order:

  1. Finish your bumroll - it will be needed for fitting the skirts
  2. Start construction on your petticoat. See "Petticoat Party"
  3. If you are really gung ho, start construction on the kirtle skirt as well--it is the exact same process as the petticoat and may save time with fitting and such later on
  4. Complete your corset
  5. Complete your smock
  6. Cut the bodice out of linen
  7. Sew the bodice

Petticoat Party

When

TBD

TBD

Where

Goal of Session

Learn how to make the skirts: How to do different types of pleats, how to fit the skirts, and how to sew it to the waistband. If we have time, let's also cut out the bumrolls this session.

Before Session

  • Have your bumroll completed and bring it. You'll need it for the skirt fitting!
  • Decide what kind of pleating you want to use
  • Start the skirt ahead of time. Cut out a 3 yard length of your petticoat fabric. Sew the short edges together, so you have a tube. The tube should be 3 yards in circumference and 55" tall. Hem one edge of this tube, which will be the bottom edge. This will make it so at the session we can fit the skirts during the session (excess fabric is cut of of the top, not the bottom.) and get started with the pleating. For more info see the petticoat page on elizabethaconstume.net
